Direct Debit lets a customer authorise you once, then pay on demand without approving each charge. You create a contract, the customer signs it in their wallet, and from then on you charge it whenever you need to — for subscriptions, metered usage, or top-ups.

For the endpoint reference and a live playground, see [Create contract](/api/v1/reference/create-direct-debit-contract) and the other Direct Debit endpoints in the sidebar.

<Note>
  Direct Debit is currently live on **Binance Pay** (`BINANCE_PAY`). Bybit and KuCoin scenario codes are reserved but not yet enabled.
</Note>

---

## Contract Lifecycle

<Steps>
  <Step title="Look up a scenario code">
    Call `GET /v1/direct-debit/scenario-code/list` to fetch the scenarios your account can use. A scenario defines the service type and its per-transaction ceiling. Cache the result — it changes rarely.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the contract">
    Call `POST /v1/direct-debit` with the scenario, a per-charge limit, and your return and cancel URLs. The response carries a `qrContent` and a `deepLink`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the customer to sign">
    Present the QR code, or redirect to the deep link on mobile. The customer approves the contract in their wallet and lands on your `returnUrl`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the signature">
    Wait for the contract webhook, or call `POST /v1/direct-debit/{id}/sync` to pull the latest status from the provider. The contract is chargeable once it reaches `SIGNED`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Charge on demand">
    Call `POST /v1/direct-debit/{id}/payment` whenever you need to collect. No customer interaction is involved, so the response has no QR code or checkout link.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To bill on a fixed schedule instead of charging manually, attach a [Subscription](/api/v1/reference/create-subscription) to the signed contract and CeyPay runs the billing cycle for you.

## Contract Statuses

| Status       | Meaning                                             |
| ------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| `INITIATED`  | Created, awaiting the customer's signature          |
| `SIGNED`     | Signed and ready to charge                          |
| `TERMINATED` | Cancelled by you or the customer                    |
| `EXPIRED`    | The signing window lapsed before the customer signed |

Only `SIGNED` contracts can be charged or terminated.

## Currency and Slippage

A contract is denominated in either `USDT` or `LKR`, but you can charge it in **either** currency regardless of how it was created. Every charge is converted to USDT before it reaches the provider.

### Why LKR contracts need a buffer

The provider only understands USDT limits. When you create an LKR contract, CeyPay converts your LKR limit to USDT at the current rate and sends that figure to Binance. If the exchange rate moves before you charge, the same LKR amount may now convert to more USDT than the contract allows — and the charge is rejected.

`slippageBps` inflates the USDT limit sent to the provider to absorb that movement.

<Warning>
  Slippage applies to LKR contracts only. Sending `slippageBps` on a `USDT` contract is rejected with a `400`.
</Warning>

### Worked example

```
Contract:      1000 LKR, slippageBps 10000 (100%)
Exchange rate: 1 USDT = 330 LKR

1. Base conversion:  1000 LKR ÷ 330  = 3.03 USDT
2. Apply slippage:   3.03 × (1 + 1.0) = 6.06 USDT
3. Limit sent to provider:             6.06 USDT
```

That contract then accepts LKR charges up to roughly 2000 LKR while the rate holds at 330, or USDT charges up to 6.06 USDT.

### Choosing a buffer

| Basis points | Buffer | Use when                                      |
| ------------ | ------ | --------------------------------------------- |
| `0`          | 0%     | You charge immediately after signing          |
| `5000`       | 50%    | Charges land within days                      |
| `10000`      | 100%   | Long-lived contracts, monthly billing         |
| `20000`      | 200%   | Maximum permitted buffer                      |

The buffer raises the ceiling, not the amount charged. A larger buffer costs you nothing — it only widens the range of charges the contract will accept.

### Charge validation

Every charge is converted to USDT at the current rate and checked against the contract's buffered limit. Charges over the limit are rejected with a message showing the conversion:

```json
{
  "statusCode": 400,
  "message": "Payment 5000 LKR converts to 15.15 USDT, exceeding contract limit 6.06 USDT.",
  "error": "Bad Request"
}
```

On a response, `singleUpperLimit` is always the USDT figure sent to the provider. For LKR contracts, `singleUpperLimitLkr` carries the original LKR amount you specified.

## Webhooks

Set `webhookUrl` when creating a contract to receive contract and charge events. A `webhookUrl` on an individual charge overrides the contract's for that charge only.

See the [Webhooks Guide](/api/v1/webhooks) for payload formats, signature verification, and the retry schedule.
